# Decision: Archer Deck Positioning

**Date:** April 2026
**Decision:** Frame the deck as researchers seeking feedback, not founders pitching.

## What we decided
Tone of deck = "We're UIUC engineers researching the fleet ops software gap. Does this problem exist at Archer? How are you solving it? Would you talk to us?"

Not: "We're building X, here's our pitch."

## Why
- Rushil's dad is a manager, not a decision-maker. He's forwarding the deck, not evaluating it.
- A pitch to a manager reads as presumptuous. A research inquiry reads as credible.
- The ask on the last slide is: "who's the right person to talk to?" — easy yes for anyone who sees it.
- We don't have a product yet. Positioning as researchers is accurate and appropriate for pre-seed.

## What we rejected
- Aggressive pitch deck positioning ("here's why you should partner with us")
- Full investor-style deck (wrong audience — this is an industry contact, not an investor)

## Next step
Write the concept email with the same tone. Keep it short — 4–5 sentences max.
