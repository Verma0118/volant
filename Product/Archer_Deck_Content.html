Slide 2 — The Analogy
The Market Parallel

Samsara solved this for trucking.
We don't think aviation has its equivalent yet.

Samsara is a $25B company that built fleet telematics, route optimization, compliance tracking, and dispatch tools for truck operators. The problem they solved for trucking is the same problem autonomous aviation operators are about to face — but no equivalent platform exists for aircraft.

Capability Samsara — Trucking Aviation Today
Fleet visibility Real-time GPS dashboard, status per vehicle Fragmented — ADS-B readers, OEM portals, manual logs. No unified view.
Maintenance tracking Automated alerts, mileage-based scheduling Spreadsheets or MRO tools built for fixed-wing aircraft, not eVTOL battery cycles
Compliance logging Automated HOS and DOT reporting Manual FAA documentation — Part 135 records, incident logs done by hand
Dispatch Route optimization, driver assignment Ad hoc — phone calls, internal spreadsheets, no deconfliction tooling
Platform status $25B purpose-built company Purpose-built aviation equivalent: does not appear to exist.
The competitive landscape includes FlytBase, AlarisPro, AirData UAV, and Aloft — none architected for both drone fleets today and eVTOL at commercial scale. OEMs like Archer are investing in software (Palantir, NVIDIA IGX Thor, SpaceX Starlink) — but those investments target autonomy, flight controls, and onboard AI. The fleet operations management layer — dispatch, maintenance tracking, compliance logging — remains unsolved. The gap is real.
Slide 3 — The Problem
What Gets Hard at Scale

Operating 30 aircraft is not just 30x harder than operating one.
It's a different category of problem.

When an operator is managing a fleet in commercial service, six problems appear simultaneously — none of which existing software handles well in an aviation context:

1. Fleet visibility

Knowing which aircraft are in-flight, charging, in maintenance, or grounded across an entire operation — in real time. Not refreshing a spreadsheet. Not calling a crew chief.

2. Battery and power cycle management

eVTOL-specific: tracking state of charge, cycle count, and battery degradation across a fleet. Scheduling around charging infrastructure. Flagging aircraft approaching replacement thresholds before they fail mid-mission.

3. Maintenance tracking

Flight-hour-based maintenance scheduling, unscheduled incident logging, parts inventory, airworthiness documentation — all tied to FAA-compliant records. Not adapted from fixed-wing MRO software.

4. Regulatory compliance

FAA Part 135 reporting for air taxi operations, LAANC airspace authorization (currently 5–10 min manual paperwork, should be ~1.4 seconds automated), pilot certification tracking, audit trail for every flight.

5. Mission dispatch

Assigning aircraft to routes based on battery level, location, airspace availability, and crew readiness. Handling deconfliction when multiple aircraft are operating in the same airspace. Automated re-routing on weather holds.

6. Utilization analytics

Cost per flight hour, aircraft downtime rates, revenue per aircraft, fleet utilization — the financial data operators need to know if their operation is profitable. Currently calculated manually if at all.

Slide 4 — Market Timing
Why Now

The window to build this right is before commercial operations begin — not during.

Now — 2025
Flight testing, certification, pre-commercial ops. Fleets are small and manageable manually. The software gap isn't painful yet — but the infrastructure decisions are being made right now.
2026 — Now
First commercial eVTOL routes are beginning this year. Archer is operating Midnight in the U.S. (FAA eIPP program) and UAE. 10–30 aircraft per operator. Manual ops processes become a liability. Operators without purpose-built tooling are flying blind.
2028 — 2030
Fleet scaling. 100+ aircraft per operator. Software built for small fleets doesn't scale. Operators who built the right infrastructure early have a significant operational and cost advantage.
Today (Bridge)
Drone fleet operators — commercial inspection, delivery, enterprise — are already running fleets of 20–200 aircraft and facing this exact problem. Same software, smaller aircraft. Solving it for drones now validates the platform before eVTOL scale arrives.

Slide 5 — The Product Concept
What We're Building

A single operator dashboard that gives aviation ops teams real-time control of their entire fleet.

Think of it as the operations center software layer — not an avionics system, not a booking platform. The tool that the dispatch manager, maintenance coordinator, and operations director use to run the day-to-day.

Core views (MVP hypothesis)

ViewWhat it doesWhy it matters
Fleet Overview All aircraft, real-time status: in-flight, charging, maintenance, grounded Operators need a single source of truth for fleet state
Mission Dispatch Assign aircraft to routes, manage crew, handle holds and re-routing Manual dispatch doesn't scale past ~5 aircraft
Maintenance Tracker Flight hours, battery cycles, scheduled vs. unscheduled maintenance, work orders Predictive maintenance is one of the highest-value ops tools in aviation
Compliance Log FAA Part 135/107 records, LAANC authorizations, incident logs, audit trail Compliance can't be an afterthought — it needs to be built in from day one
Analytics Cost per flight hour, utilization rates, downtime, fleet revenue performance Operators flying blind on unit economics won't survive commercial scale

Business model hypothesis

SaaS subscription: $200–500 per aircraft per month. Basis: Samsara's full-platform fleet management runs ~$100–150/vehicle/month for trucking. Aviation operators carry significantly higher compliance, airspace, and safety liability — the platform value justifies a premium over trucking equivalents. Unvalidated — this is what we're testing with operators.

Technical approach

React + Mapbox frontend. Node.js/Express API. Apache Kafka for high-throughput telemetry streaming. Redis for live state. PostgreSQL + TimescaleDB for structured records and battery history. MAVLink for drones. ADS-B + OEM API integration for eVTOL. Built on AWS with SOC2 compliance in mind from the start.

Slide 7 — The Ask
Why We're Reaching Out

Three questions — and an open door if any of this resonates.

01
Does Archer have — or plan to build — purpose-built fleet operations software?
We're aware Archer has partnered with Palantir, NVIDIA, and SpaceX on software — but those appear to cover autonomy, flight controls, and connectivity. We're asking specifically about the fleet operations layer: dispatch, maintenance tracking, FAA compliance logging. Is that being built in-house, sourced externally, or not yet prioritized?

If it's being built in-house, is there appetite to work with an outside team on specific components — or to pilot a third-party platform?
We're not assuming yes. Some operators will want full control and build everything internally. Others will want to focus engineering resources on the aircraft and partner for ops software. We're genuinely asking which camp Archer is in.
03
Who at Archer would be the right person to continue this conversation?
We're looking for whoever owns the fleet operations or digital technology roadmap — someone like the Director of Digital Technology Program Management or a senior engineer on the ops software side. Even a 20-minute conversation would help us build the right thing.
