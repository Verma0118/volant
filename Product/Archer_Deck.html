The Problem Space
Operating aircraft at scale is fundamentally
different from operating one aircraft.
  • Fleet status at a glance — knowing which aircraft are in flight, charging, in maintenance, or grounded across an entire operation simultaneously
  • Battery & power cycle management — eVTOL-specific: tracking charge state, cycle count, degradation across a fleet, scheduling around charging infrastructure
  • Maintenance tracking — flight-hour-based scheduling, unscheduled incident logging, parts inventory, airworthiness documentation
  • FAA compliance logging — Part 135 reporting, pilot certifications, airworthiness records, incident documentation — all audit-ready
  • Mission dispatch — assigning aircraft to routes, managing crew, handling weather holds and re-routing at scale
  • Utilization analytics — cost per flight hour, downtime rates, revenue per aircraft — the operational data operators need to be profitable

Market Timing
The window to build this right is before commercial
operations begin — not during.
Now — 2025
Flight testing, certification, pre-commercial ops. Small fleets, manageable manually. The software gap isn't painful yet.
2025 — 2027
First commercial routes (LA, NYC, Dubai). 10–30 aircraft per operator. Manual ops become a liability. The software need becomes urgent.
2028 — 2030
Fleet scaling. 100+ aircraft. Operators who don't have purpose-built software by this point are flying blind.
Today
Drone fleet operators already face this problem at scale. They're the proof of concept — and the bridge market.
Why drones matter: Commercial drone operators — inspection, delivery, enterprise — are running fleets of 20–200 aircraft today. The ops software problem is identical to eVTOL, just with smaller aircraft. Solving it for drones now validates the platform for eVTOL at scale.

Who We Are
Two UIUC engineers building toward
an aviation-native operations platform.
  • Aerospace engineering background — we understand the domain: airworthiness requirements, FAA certification pathways, the difference between fixed-wing and eVTOL maintenance profiles
  • We've identified the gap — existing fleet software is either built for trucking (Samsara, Motive), generic enterprise ops tools, or OEM-proprietary. Nothing purpose-built for autonomous aviation operations
  • Early-stage concept — we're at the research and validation stage. We're not here to sell anything. We want to understand how leading companies are thinking about this before we build the wrong thing.
  • Focused on the operator — dispatch managers, operations centers, maintenance coordinators. The people running the day-to-day, not the pilots.
Our research question:

Does purpose-built fleet operations software exist for eVTOL operators today — or is this problem being solved with internal tools, adapted trucking software, or manual processes?

We believe the honest answer to this question shapes everything about what gets built next.
