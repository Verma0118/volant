# Volant — Prospect Company List
*Researched May 16, 2026. Source: web search + industry data.*

Priority tiers based on: operational status, fleet scale, pain fit, and accessibility.

---

## TIER 1 — Outreach Now (operational + clear pain)

### 1. Joby Aviation
- **Why:** Commercial ops launching 2026. First NYC eVTOL flight completed Apr 27. Acquired Avionyx (aero software firm) for FAA cert — signals they build some ops software internally but not everything. Bonny Simi is President of Operations.
- **Fleet:** ~10 FAA-conforming aircraft now, ramping to 4/month by 2027, 500/year by ~2030.
- **Target roles:** VP/Director of Operations Technology, Head of Fleet Operations
- **Warm path:** UIUC aerospace faculty → Joby hiring pipeline (Joby is a UIUC-heavy shop). LinkedIn alumni search.
- **Risk:** May build in-house. Acquisitions signal preference to own the stack. Go as a research partner first, not vendor pitch.

### 2. Zipline
- **Why:** Most operationally mature drone company in the world. 2M+ deliveries, 125M+ autonomous miles. FAA expanded their Part 135 ops to 400 flights/day (signed Mar 24, 2026). $800M raised in Jan+Mar 2026. Expanding to Houston, Phoenix, Salt Lake City, Cleveland, Jacksonville, Long Island, Rochester.
- **Fleet:** Hundreds of Platform 2 VTOL drones across US + international
- **Target roles:** Head of Fleet Operations, VP of Operations Technology, Director of Maintenance
- **Warm path:** Cold LinkedIn outreach. They're growing fast — ops hires visible on LinkedIn.
- **Fit:** Maintenance tracking, compliance logging, and utilization analytics are exactly their pain at this scale. They likely have internal tools but at 400 flights/day, gaps exist.

### 3. Wing (Alphabet)
- **Why:** 450K+ deliveries. Expanding to 150 Walmart stores by 2027. Real fleet, real regulatory compliance burden.
- **Fleet:** Large, exact number not public. Multi-city US operations.
- **Target roles:** Director of Operations, Fleet Technology lead
- **Warm path:** UIUC → Google/Alphabet alumni network on LinkedIn. Harder to crack given Alphabet resources.
- **Fit:** FAA compliance logging and utilization analytics are the strongest hooks — Wing flies Part 135, audit trail is mandatory.

### 4. Eve Air Mobility (Embraer subsidiary)
- **Why:** Contracted to deliver 60 eVTOL aircraft to Blade starting 2026. Embraer backing = serious OEM. Blade's passenger ops were acquired by Joby, so Eve's next customers need identifying.
- **Fleet:** Pre-commercial but aircraft delivering to operators NOW.
- **Target roles:** Director of Operations Technology, Head of Fleet Services
- **Warm path:** LinkedIn → Embraer aerospace alumni. UIUC has Embraer connections through aerospace dept.
- **Fit:** They need fleet ops software BEFORE their operators do — position as the ops platform to bundle with aircraft sales.

## TIER 2 — Research First, Then Outreach

### 5. Beta Technologies
- **Why:** Building ALIA aircraft + nationwide charging network. Customers: UPS, United Therapeutics, Air New Zealand. Unique position: they're an OEM + infrastructure company. Fleet ops software is a gap they'll need.
- **Fleet:** Pre-commercial. UPS delivery ops likely first.
- **Target roles:** Director of Digital Operations, Head of Fleet Technology
- **Warm path:** Cold outreach. Vermont-based, smaller team, more accessible than Joby.

### 6. Percepto (CHANNEL opportunity)
- **Why:** Sells drone-in-a-box to Fortune 500 industrial customers (Verizon, Enel, Florida Power & Light, Koch Industries, Siemens Energy, Delek US). FAA approval for 1 pilot to run 30 simultaneous drones. Their customers ARE fleet operators who need Volant-style software — Percepto could be a distribution channel, not a direct customer.
- **Target:** Explore a partnership where Volant is the fleet ops layer and Percepto is the hardware layer.
- **Contact approach:** Founder/BD level conversation. Israeli company (Tel Aviv HQ), US operations team.

### 7. Wisk Aero (Boeing subsidiary)
- **Why:** Boeing money, long timeline (2030 commercial launch), autonomous focus. Early infrastructure decisions are being made now. Boeing is a known buyer of enterprise ops software.
- **Target roles:** Director of Operations Software, Fleet Systems architect
- **Warm path:** Boeing alumni on LinkedIn. UIUC aerospace has Boeing connections.
- **Risk:** Boeing may mandate internal tooling. Go as a research conversation.

### 8. Amazon Prime Air
- **Why:** Part 135 certified, active US deliveries. Amazon won't buy third-party software but the conversation is worth having to understand the market.
- **Fit:** Low. Amazon builds everything internally.
- **Action:** Skip for now, revisit if they signal openness to third-party tools.

## TIER 3 — Bridge Market (Commercial Drone Fleet Operators)

### 9. Pyka
- **Why:** Agricultural spray drones, fleet operations for row crops. Operational now in US + international. ~20-50 aircraft fleets per client.
- **Fit:** Maintenance tracking + flight-hour accrual are perfect fits for agricultural spray drones (FAA Part 137 compliance burden).
- **Warm path:** LinkedIn cold.

### 10. Skydio Enterprise Customers (indirect)
- **Why:** Skydio sells hardware to utilities (ComEd expanded to Skydio X10), law enforcement, DoD. Their customers are the fleet operators. Skydio themselves won't buy software — but their customers (ComEd, etc.) might.
- **Action:** Identify 3-5 major utility companies running Skydio fleets → reach out to their drone ops managers directly.

### 11. FAA Part 135 UAS Operators (cold list)
- **Why:** FAA has issued ~15 Part 135 UAS certifications (12 new in 2025, 15-18 expected in 2026). Every one of them has a legally mandated maintenance program and audit trail requirement.
- **Action:** FAA public records → pull the list of Part 135 UAS operators → LinkedIn → drone ops manager at each.
- **Fit:** PERFECT. Compliance logging is literally a regulatory requirement for them.
