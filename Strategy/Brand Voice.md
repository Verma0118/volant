## Voice in One Line
Researcher's precision + founder's conviction. Never pitching, always explaining. Short sentences. No hedging.

---

## Core Patterns (from source material)

### Sentence structure
- Lead with the problem or the frame, not the solution
- Two-sentence max per point — state it, then justify it once
- "This is X. Here's why that matters." — not "We believe X could potentially..."
- Questions are rhetorical tools, not uncertainty: "Does aviation have its Samsara? We don't think so."

### What to never say
- "We believe" / "we think" / "we feel" — state it or don't
- "Potentially" / "could be" / "might" — removes conviction
- "Excited to share" / "happy to connect" — generic opener, kills credibility
- Adjective-first claims: "innovative platform" / "cutting-edge solution" — earn the adjective with a fact instead

### Tone anchors
- **Researcher, not founder**: "We're studying this problem" beats "We built this product"
- **Domain-fluent without being jargon-heavy**: Use Part 135, LAANC, deconfliction — don't explain them to people who know them
- **Honest about stage**: "Pre-seed, no prototype, talking to operators before we build the wrong thing" — doesn't oversell

### Numbers handling
- Lead with the comparison that lands: "$25B Samsara for trucking" before "$9.45B eVTOL software market"
- Flag unvalidated figures: "Hypothesis: $200–500/aircraft/month — unvalidated"
- Source everything in investor materials; in outreach, cite the analogy not the number

## Voice Samples (from vault)

**Good (from deck Slide 2):**
> "Samsara is a $25B company that built fleet telematics, route optimization, compliance tracking, and dispatch tools for truck operators. The problem they solved for trucking is the same problem autonomous aviation operators are about to face — but no equivalent platform exists for aircraft."

**Good (from email draft):**
> "We're two UIUC aerospace engineers who believe the fleet operations software gap in eVTOL is going to be a significant problem for operators at scale — the same way it was for trucking before Samsara."

**Good (from Slide 6):**
> "We're not here to pitch a finished product. We're here because getting in front of the right people at the right companies before everyone is flying at scale is the only way to build something actually useful."

## Application Rules

| Channel | Tone adjustment |
|---------|----------------|
| Cold email to industry contact | Researcher tone, 5 sentences max, single concrete ask |
| Investor cold email | Researcher + traction signals, lead with analogy, end with specific ask |
| Pitch deck | Build the case with facts, let the analogy do the persuasion, be explicit about what's unvalidated |
| LinkedIn / social | Shorter version of deck voice — observation + implication, not announcement |
