# Volant — YC/VC Investment Case Analysis

> 4-voice council output (Architect + Skeptic + Pragmatist + Critic). Run: Apr 29, 2026.

---

## Council Positions

**Architect:** Real structural opportunity, earned moat doesn't exist yet. Bridge strategy is correct sequencing but OEM API access is existential and must be de-risked early through operator relationships, not OEM relationships.

**Skeptic:** Solution in search of distribution. TAM is borrowed — actual SAM today is sub-$50M. "Samsara for aviation" is a comp, not a moat. Bridge strategy teaches the wrong GTM muscle because drone ≠ eVTOL buyer persona.

**Pragmatist:** Right positioning, zero validation. Get 3-5 paying drone operators in 60 days — if you can't close anyone, the eVTOL thesis doesn't matter. Archer is the wrong ICP at this stage. The signed contract IS the moat — even at $99/month, a signature tells YC you're a company.

**Critic:** Unit economics are brutal at small fleet sizes. 10-aircraft customer = $1K–$5K MRR. Bridge strategy risks building 2 years of technical and GTM debt optimized for the wrong end customer. Existing competitors (FlytBase, DroneDeploy, Airdata) already serve drone operators well enough.

---

## Verdict

- **Consensus:** No moat exists yet. SAM today ≈ $50-100M. OEM API lockout is the biggest structural risk. Pricing hypothesis unvalidated.
- **Strongest dissent:** Pragmatist vs. Skeptic on bridge. Pragmatist wins — prove the bridge in 60 days or the eVTOL thesis is fiction.
- **Premise check:** Skeptic challenged root question — distribution is the variable, not product.
- **Recommendation:** Stop optimizing the product. Go sell. Target ICP: drone delivery or inspection company, 20+ aircraft. Get one signed contract before YC application.

> SOM calculation: 20 customers × avg 30 aircraft × $300/aircraft/month = $2.16M ARR. Achievable.

### Moat
Nothing today. Path:
1. **Workflow lock-in** — deep dispatch integration = ripping Volant out = operational crisis
2. **Data flywheel** — operational data across many operators → predictive features nobody else has
3. **Compliance layer stickiness** — FAA records / LAANC logs become hard to migrate
Timeline: 12-24 months to first real moat.

### ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
**Wrong:** Archer, Joby, OEMs (18-month sales cycle, near-zero close probability pre-revenue)
**Right:** Regional drone delivery or infrastructure inspection company
- 20-50 aircraft
- Dispatcher manually tracking missions in spreadsheets
- Has felt a missed compliance window
- Has a technical POC with budget authority
- Can sign a 12-month contract

### GTM (Go-to-Market)
- **Now:** Founder-led direct sales. LinkedIn DMs to drone fleet ops managers.
- **After 3 customers:** Design partner program → case studies → open door to Series A
- **Wrong path:** OEM enterprise sales before any revenue

### Unit Economics Hypothesis
| Fleet size | MRR @ $300/aircraft | ARR |
|---|---|---|
| 10 aircraft | $3,000 | $36,000 |
| 30 aircraft | $9,000 | $108,000 |
| 50 aircraft | $15,000 | $180,000 |

- Target 20+ aircraft customers minimum
- Price on fleet size, not per-seat
- LTV/CAC > 3x required. Founder-led sales = near-zero CAC. Hire sales rep and model breaks.
- Churn < 5%/year = LTV of $50K+ on 30-aircraft customer. Works.

### Defensibility
- Today: 0
- After 3 deep integrations: switching cost layer
- After 10 customers: proprietary flight ops dataset → prediction features → real lock-in

### Burn / Runway
- Two students, no salaries, no funding = near-zero burn
- 6-12 months to find first paying customer before fundraise pressure
- Biggest advantage: time
